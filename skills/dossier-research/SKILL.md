# Research a person dossier

Load `sourcing-rules` first. Dossier tickets (e.g. fsspx#7–#17, tl#6–#20)
share a standard checklist; the work:

1. **Verified biography.** Birth, formation, ordination/appointments (with
   who/when/where for episcopal consecrations — lineage matters), offices,
   death. Every date sourced; conflicts between sources recorded, not
   resolved by preference.
2. **Works and acts.** Principal writings with publication data (original
   language + translations); the key documents they signed or provoked.
3. **Institutional reception of the person specifically.** Notification?
   Silencing? Excommunication? Nothing? Dated and attributed — outcomes
   differ sharply between figures the public lumps together.
4. **Controversies, attributed.** Each characterization tied to who makes it.
   Note where the subject's own account differs from critics' and from
   official records — all three can be cited side by side.
5. **Local relevance.** For this org: the Brazil thread (Portuguese-language
   sources are first-class, not supplementary).
6. **Output.** Corrections/enrichment to `figures[]`, `events[]`,
   `references[]` (and lineage/genealogy structures where present) in a PR
   that keeps validate/tests/build green; tick the ticket's boxes with links;
   flag volatile sources for archiving.

Quality bar: a dossier is done when someone hostile to the subject and someone
sympathetic to them would both accept the page as accurate.
